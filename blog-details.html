The Secret to the Perfect Wagyu Sear

Cooking authentic A5 Japanese Wagyu is an art form. Unlike standard steaks, Wagyu contains intense intramuscular fat marbling that melts at entirely different temperatures. If handled incorrectly, the steak loses its signature buttery texture and umami richness.

At Stackly, our master chefs follow a strict, disciplined ritual every evening before service begins. We use specialized Bincho-tan white charcoal imported straight from Japan to guarantee stable, smokeless searing heat.

"The secret is not just the fire. It is recognizing the exact moment the fat begins to render, and relying on absolute patience."

The Temperature Control

Many amateur chefs make the mistake of dropping cold Wagyu directly onto a screaming hot pan. We ensure every cut is brought to exactly room temperature over the course of two hours. We season solely with coarse sea salt — no pepper, no marinades, and absolutely no oils during the initial sear.

Wagyu carries its own fat. We let the beef render its own natural tallow, locking in unparalleled flavor profiles and creating that sought-after caramelized, crispy crust while preserving a perfectly velvet interior.
